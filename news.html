October 4, 2024

Dandadan

~ The anime Dan Da Dan, adapted from Yukinobu Tatsu's popular manga, recently launched on streaming platforms like Netflix, Hulu, and Crunchyroll. This supernatural comedy-drama follows the journey of two high school students, Momo Ayase, who comes from a family of spirit mediums, and her classmate Ken Takakura (nicknamed "Okarun"). While Momo is a skeptic about aliens but believes in ghosts, Okarun has the opposite beliefs, leading them into an unusual bet to prove each other wrong. To settle their debate, they venture separately into a haunted tunnel and an abandoned hospital, expecting to disprove each other’s beliefs. However, they encounter both spirits and aliens, setting off a chaotic adventure that draws them closer while awakening their latent supernatural abilities.

~ Dan Da Dan balances horror elements with humor and romance, as Momo and Okarun confront bizarre beings like "Turbo Granny" and other yōkai, as well as aliens. The storyline includes thrilling battles and quirky supernatural interactions, making it a standout with fresh comedic twists and intense action. This anime is helmed by director Fuga Yamashiro, with vibrant animation by the acclaimed Science Saru studio, bringing a lively and bizarre world to life.

~ For those following the manga, the anime covers events up to chapter 31, which includes fan-favorite characters like the sinister "Evil Eye" and others with eccentric powers and backgrounds. Dan Da Dan is set to appeal to fans of supernatural adventure with its distinctive mix of horror, humor, and heartfelt friendship

October 4, 2024

Blue Box

~ The newly released anime Blue Box brings a refreshing take on romance mixed with sports, focusing on Taiki Inomata, a determined first-year badminton player, and his crush, Chinatsu Kano, an older basketball player. Set in Eimei Junior and Senior High, a school known for its athletics, Blue Box explores the dynamics of young love and ambition when these two characters unexpectedly start living under the same roof. Their shared dedication to sports and growing romantic tension create an engaging blend of youthful passion and aspiration.

~ Adapted from Kōji Miura’s manga, Blue Box skillfully balances both romance and sports, adding depth to each through its animation and soundtrack. The visuals have been particularly praised for capturing intense sports action, with fluid movements and powerful close-ups that add to the story's emotional weight. Music also plays a key role, with themes by Official HIGE DANDism and Eve setting the tone for both triumph and introspection​.

~ Blue Box is currently streaming on Netflix, making it accessible to a broad audience and a notable addition to fall anime offerings. This series promises to develop further as both Taiki and Chinatsu navigate the highs and lows of their personal and athletic lives, appealing to fans of romance and slice-of-life anime alike.

july 4, 2024

Days with My Stepsister

~ Days with My Stepsister, also known as Gimai Seikatsu, is a slice-of-life anime series exploring the dynamics between two high school students who become step-siblings under unexpected circumstances. The story begins when Yuuta, a reserved and introspective boy, finds his life turned upside down after his father remarries. He gains a stepsister, Saki, whose cheerful personality contrasts with his quiet demeanor. Both characters struggle to navigate this new relationship, starting as strangers bound by family ties and slowly growing closer over shared experiences, typical teenage struggles, and quiet, introspective moments.

~ The show’s focus lies in its character-driven storytelling, centering on subtle, everyday interactions that gradually reveal the complexities of their personalities. Yuuta and Saki’s bond grows through small acts of care and empathy, fostering a mutual understanding that goes beyond mere friendship yet remains just short of romance. The series emphasizes a slow-paced, realistic portrayal of blended family relationships, and unlike typical romantic dramas, it maintains a grounded and gentle approach.

~ Produced by Studio Deen, Days with My Stepsister aired from July to September 2024 and was well-received for its introspective atmosphere and its break from romance tropes, offering instead a nuanced look at step-sibling dynamics. With its heartfelt approach, the series appeals to fans of slow-paced, character-focused storytelling in anime.
